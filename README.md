# Melody Incipit Grid Tool

**Break down any melody fast.**

The Melody Incipit Grid Tool gives you a clear, repeatable way to map a melody across **chords, counts, scale degrees, and lyrics or syllables**.

Use it to learn a song, transcribe a phrase, study a hook, practice a difficult passage, compare melodic patterns, or document a melody of your own.

**Live tool:**  
https://danielleackerman.github.io/melody-incipit-tool/

## 🎼 What the Tool Helps You Do

A melody can feel obvious when you hear it but become harder to understand when you try to sing, play, remember, or teach it.

This tool breaks the melody into aligned layers so you can see:

- Which chord is underneath each bar
- Where each note falls in the count
- How the melody moves through scale degrees
- Which lyric or syllable belongs to each note
- How several bars connect into a phrase

Instead of treating the melody as one large thing to memorize, you can work through it one bar and one subdivision at a time.

## ✨ Ways to Use It

### Learn a song

Map a vocal or instrumental phrase while listening to a recording. Add the chord, count the rhythm, identify the scale degrees, and align the lyrics or syllables.

### Practice a difficult section

Isolate a short passage and see exactly where the notes and words land. Slow the learning process down without losing the musical structure.

### Transcribe by ear

Use scale degrees instead of staff notation to record what you hear quickly. The grid helps separate pitch movement, rhythm, harmony, and lyrics.

### Study melody patterns

Compare openings, hooks, verse phrases, cadences, repeated motifs, and melodic contours across different songs.

### Write and develop melodies

Document your own melody in a form that is easy to review, revise, compare, and return to later.

### Teach or explain a phrase

Create a readable map that shows another singer or musician how the parts of the melody fit together.

## 🧭 How It Works

1. Enter the title and any useful context, such as key, mode, meter, tempo, feel, or audio reference.
2. Choose the rhythmic grid: quarter notes, eighth notes, or sixteenth notes.
3. Add the chord for each bar.
4. Enter the scale degree in each subdivision.
5. Align the lyric, word, or syllable beneath the corresponding note.
6. Review the melody in the live aligned preview.
7. Export or copy the result for your notes, practice materials, or song archive.

## 🧩 The Four Main Lanes

### Chords

Shows the harmonic context beneath the melody.

### Counts

Shows where each note or syllable falls in time.

### Scale Degrees

Records the melody relative to the key:

- `1` is the tonal center
- `3` is the third degree
- `5` is the fifth degree
- `b7` becomes `♭7`
- `#4` becomes `♯4`
- `-` can mark a held note

Scale degrees make it easier to understand and reuse the shape of a melody in different keys.

### Lyrics or Syllables

Places each word or syllable directly beneath the note where it occurs.

For example:

```text
Chords:  | C                         | F                         |
Counts:  | 1   &   2   &   3   &   4   & | 1   &   2   &   3   &   4   & |
Degrees: | 3       3   2   1       5     | 4       3   2   1             |
Lyrics:  | I       was walk- ing   home  | call-   ing Your name          |
```

The tool handles the spacing automatically so the musical information remains aligned.

## 🎯 A Different Way to Learn Melody

Traditional notation is powerful, but it is not always the fastest way to understand a short melodic phrase.

A voice memo lets you hear the melody but does not show how it is organized.

Lyrics alone do not show pitch or rhythm.

Chord sheets show harmony but usually not the exact melody.

The Melody Incipit Grid Tool brings those layers together in one compact view. It is not intended to replace notation, recordings, or instrumental practice. It gives you another way to **break the learning process into visible, manageable parts**.

## 🎹 Capture Mode and Read Mode

### Capture Mode

Use the musical bar cards to enter and revise the melody.

You can:

- Add, duplicate, clear, delete, and reorder bars
- Create more than one phrase
- Change the rhythmic resolution
- Enter notes and lyrics with the keyboard
- Watch the preview update as you work

### Read Mode

Switch to a cleaner view for studying, reviewing, printing, or using the melody as a practice reference.

## 💾 Saving Your Work

The current melody is saved automatically in your browser.

For a durable copy, export the melody as:

- **Markdown** for Obsidian, notes, and human-readable archives
- **JSON** for reopening the complete structured melody later
- **Plain text** for quick copying, sharing, or printing

Because browser storage can be cleared, JSON export is the safest long-term backup.
